# Aquaculture Species Profiles: Technical & Economic Data

The following profiles provide water quality parameters, farm construction guidelines, and a detailed 1-Hectare economic profit/loss breakdown for major aquaculture species in India, matching the structure of the *Freshwater Prawn (Scampi)* guide.

## 1. Indian Major Carp (Rohu & Catla)
Indian Major Carps (IMC) are typically cultured together in a composite farming system. 

**Water Quality:** 
- **pH:** 7.0 to 8.5
- **Temperature:** 25°C to 32°C
- **Dissolved Oxygen (DO):** > 5.0 ppm

**Farm Construction (Pond):** 
- **Depth:** 1.5 to 2.0 meters.
- **Soil:** Clay or silt-clay loam to retain water effectively.

## 2. Vannamei Shrimp (Litopenaeus vannamei)
Vannamei represents the most profitable, highly-intensive export-driven aquaculture crop in India.

**Water Quality:** 
- **pH:** 7.5 to 8.5
- **Temperature:** 28°C to 32°C
- **Dissolved Oxygen (DO):** > 4.0 ppm 
- **Salinity:** 10 to 25 ppt (optimum)

**Farm Construction:** 
- **Depth:** 1.2 to 1.5 meters.
- **Infrastructure:** Requires bio-secure fencing, bird netting, and heavy mechanical aeration (Paddle wheel aerators).

## 3. Pangasius (Pangasianodon hypophthalmus)
A fast-growing, air-breathing white fish known for high-stocking density tolerance and low production costs.

**Water Quality:** 
- **pH:** 6.5 to 8.5
- **Temperature:** 26°C to 30°C
- **Dissolved Oxygen (DO):** Can survive in very low DO (< 3 ppm) due to air breathing.

**Farm Construction:** 
- **Depth:** 1.5 to 2.5 meters.
- **Soil:** High water retention clay.
